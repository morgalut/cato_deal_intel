# Transcript: CALL-001 - Renewal kickoff and regional rollout review

**Opportunity:** OPP-1001  
**Account:** ACC-2001  
**Date:** 2026-03-11  
**Source access level:** standard

Vendor AE: Thanks for joining. The goal today is to confirm the renewal path and understand what needs to be true before Northstar signs the global access renewal.

Elena Voss, CISO: The renewal is still the plan. I do not want anyone leaving this meeting thinking we are shopping the platform. But the renewal needs to show operational progress. We have too many regional exceptions, and every exception becomes a governance conversation.

Marco Devlin, VP Infrastructure: From infrastructure, the friction is ownership. The regional plants agree with the target state, then a local constraint shows up and nobody knows who can approve a temporary workaround.

Pavel Stone, Global Network Lead: The old plants are the hardest. Some have maintenance windows that are measured in minutes, not hours. If we force everything through the same migration path, we will either miss the renewal timeline or create outage risk.

Vendor Solutions Lead: It sounds like the renewal is tied to a practical exception model. Are you looking for us to document which sites move first, who approves temporary bypasses, and how exceptions get closed?

Elena Voss: Yes. And I need it framed as risk reduction, not just deployment activity. The board does not care that twenty sites were migrated if the highest-risk sites still have informal access paths.

Vendor AE: Understood. We can produce a rollout owner matrix and a risk-based sequencing plan. On commercial timing, are you still targeting May signature?

Elena Voss: Yes, if the final packet is clean. I will support the renewal, but I will not support an expansion story until the baseline controls are cleaner.

Marco Devlin: I agree. The platform is doing what we need, but we need one escalation owner for migration blockers. Last quarter we had three different threads for one site. That cannot happen during renewal.

Pavel Stone: I also want a weekly dashboard. It does not need to be beautiful. I need exceptions opened, exceptions closed, sites migrated, and sites at risk. If we have that, I can keep the regions honest.

Vendor Customer Success: We can draft the dashboard fields and the escalation path. For older appliances, do you want a temporary exception category or a separate legacy-site plan?

Pavel Stone: Separate legacy-site plan. Otherwise every region calls itself an exception.

Elena Voss: Good phrase. Please write that down. I want a plan that avoids normalizing exceptions.

Vendor AE: There was a procurement question about renewal uplift. Should we address that now?

Elena Voss: Procurement will ask, but the business conversation is about control maturity. If you bring me evidence that the renewal reduces exception risk, I can defend the uplift. If you only bring a price sheet, it will get stuck.

Marco Devlin: One caveat: do not overpromise the timeline. Some sites will need local testing. I would rather have an honest staged plan than a heroic plan nobody believes.

Vendor AE: Action items I heard: vendor sends rollout owner matrix, exception closure timeline, draft dashboard fields, and a legacy-site plan. Northstar confirms the first-wave sites and names regional approvers.

Elena Voss: Correct. Add one more: include a short executive summary that says what risk is lower after renewal. That is what I will use internally.

Pavel Stone: And make the dashboard exportable. I know that sounds small, but if I have to screenshot it every week, it will not last.

Vendor AE: Noted. We will send the first version by next week and flag any assumptions clearly.

Elena Voss: One more thing before we close. If you cite this call later, do not turn my support into unconditional approval. My position is supportive if the exception plan is credible.

Vendor AE: That is fair. We will label that as conditional support.

Marco Devlin: And the condition is not cosmetic. If the regional teams see that exceptions are still handled informally, they will keep using side channels.

Pavel Stone: The most useful evidence for me would be a list of current exception categories. Some are legitimate, like legacy equipment. Some are just habits.

Vendor Customer Success: We can include category definitions: legacy equipment, maintenance window, temporary executive access, and unresolved ownership. Then we can mark which categories should shrink.

Elena Voss: Good. Temporary executive access is sensitive. Do not put names in the executive summary. Use counts and control status.

Vendor AE: Understood. Counts and status only, no personal details.

Marco Devlin: For the first wave, choose sites where regional leadership is cooperative. If the first wave becomes a political fight, the whole renewal story gets noisy.

Elena Voss: I agree, but do not pick only easy sites. The board will ask whether the model works for operationally difficult regions.

Pavel Stone: Then pick one easy site, one moderate site, and one older site. That gives us a credible sample without gambling the renewal on the worst plant.

Vendor AE: That sounds like a strong sequencing principle. We will document it and ask you to confirm the site list.

Elena Voss: Perfect. The brief should show that we are reducing unmanaged access while being honest about the hard sites.
