# Transcript: CALL-010 - Factory connectivity discovery

**Opportunity:** OPP-1002  
**Account:** ACC-2002  
**Date:** 2026-03-14  
**Source access level:** standard

Vendor AE: The purpose of this discovery session is to understand what Meridian needs to prove before expanding the platform to additional factories.

Julian Maro, CIO: The headline is uptime. We are not trying to modernize for the sake of modernization. If a production line loses connectivity during a shift change, the plant manager does not care which dashboard says the policy was correct.

Clara Esteves, Director of Plant Systems: I am more optimistic than that, but Tomas is right about the standard. We need a staged rollout that plant teams trust.

Niko Hart, Network Operations Manager: My team is the one that gets called at 2 a.m. The current pilot is promising, but the troubleshooting view is not plant-friendly. It assumes the operator knows which layer failed.

Vendor Solutions Lead: Would a simplified incident view help? For example, site status, current path, policy applied, last failover event, and recommended next step.

Niko Hart: Yes, but it has to be reliable during failover. If the view is delayed by ten minutes, the operator will call it useless.

Julian Maro: We also need to know who owns what. Factory IT, central network, security, and the vendor cannot all point at each other.

Clara Esteves: The first two factories should be representative but not fragile. Pick one high-volume site and one site with older network equipment. If the proof only uses the easiest site, finance will reject the expansion.

Vendor AE: What are the success criteria for the proof?

Julian Maro: No unplanned production impact, clear failover visibility, and a report I can show finance. The report needs to say what improved, not just that a workshop happened.

Niko Hart: Add alert severity mapping. Right now every alert feels urgent. Plant staff need to know what can wait until morning.

Vendor Solutions Lead: We can create a severity guide and map alerts to owner groups.

Clara Esteves: Training matters too. Night shift cannot sit through a ninety-minute enablement session. Give them a five-minute runbook and a longer reference for supervisors.

Vendor AE: How should we handle commercial timing?

Julian Maro: Do not lead with commercial timing. Prove the operational case first. If the proof is real, I can work with finance on staged payments.

Clara Esteves: There is also a competitor conversation. They are simpler, or at least they look simpler. But I worry they do not handle segmentation as well.

Niko Hart: Simpler wins if our operators cannot use this. Better architecture does not matter during an outage if the steps are confusing.

Vendor Solutions Lead: That is a fair challenge. We will make the proof include operator tasks, not just architecture validation.

Julian Maro: Good. The opportunity is there. I do not want to spend another year with factory-by-factory exceptions. But I need proof that this will reduce operational noise.

Vendor AE: Action items: vendor proposes two proof factories, drafts success criteria, creates severity mapping, and outlines operator runbooks. Meridian confirms plant contacts and maintenance windows.

Clara Esteves: Include the shift supervisors early. They will tell you whether the process works.

Julian Maro: I also want the proof to include what happens when the vendor is not on the call. During the pilot everyone is attentive. After rollout, the plant team has to make the first decision.

Niko Hart: Exactly. If the recommended step is always "open a vendor case," that does not help. Sometimes the local team can check the path, confirm policy, and continue monitoring.

Vendor Solutions Lead: We can write the proof around decision points: local action, central network action, security action, and vendor escalation.

Clara Esteves: Make the handoffs explicit. Plant teams hate invisible ownership.

Julian Maro: For the executive brief, I need a balanced view. Do not say the proof is blocked because operations has questions. Operations should have questions. The issue is whether the questions are answerable.

Niko Hart: Mine are answerable. I am not against the platform. I am against rolling it out before the team knows what the alerts mean.

Vendor AE: So the buyer sentiment is not negative. It is conditional on operational clarity.

Clara Esteves: Correct. I am a champion, but I will not carry a messy rollout alone.

Vendor Solutions Lead: We will include a plant-readiness checklist with training, owner mapping, maintenance window, rollback criteria, and reporting export.

Julian Maro: Add proof evidence quality. I do not want anecdotes. I want timestamped pilot results and screenshots or exports where appropriate.

Niko Hart: And mark the export as unproven until it passes. I would rather see one yellow item than a green scorecard nobody trusts.
